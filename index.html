Frequently Asked Questions

✓
Does taurine supplementation actually work for cats?›
Yes. Taurine is an essential amino acid that cats cannot produce on their own. Studies show it supports cardiovascular function, retinal health, reproduction, and immune response. Most owners notice improvements in energy and vitality within 2–4 weeks.
✓
My cat has kidney disease (CKD). Can taurine help?›
Cats with CKD are at higher risk of taurine-related decline, especially heart complications like HCM. Taurine is essential for heart muscle function. Supplementing ensures they're not missing this critical amino acid despite a restricted diet.
✓
My cat vomits frequently. Could taurine help?›
Taurine plays a role in bile acid production, which helps your cat properly digest fats and reduces GI irritation. Many cat owners notice improved digestion and less bile vomiting after consistent supplementation.
✓
How is this different from the taurine in cat food?›
Commercial processing destroys up to 50% of naturally occurring taurine. KittySupps™ Taurine is pharmaceutical-grade and hasn't been heat-damaged or degraded — your cat receives full potency with every scoop.
✓
How often should I give KittySupps™ Taurine?›
Just one scoop daily, mixed into any food. Consistent daily supplementation is key since cats cannot produce taurine on their own.
✓
Is KittySupps™ Taurine safe for cats?›
Yes. KittySupps worked with veterinary nutritionists to ensure our Taurine supplement is suitable for cats of all sizes and breeds, 12 weeks and older.
✓
What if my cat won't eat it?›
KittySupps™ Taurine is an unflavored, odorless powder. For picky eaters, start with ⅛ scoop for 2–3 days, then gradually increase. Try mixing with tuna juice first. Over 95% of picky eater concerns are resolved this way.
✓
Can I give too much taurine? Is it safe every day?›
No, you cannot overdose on taurine. Unlike fat-soluble vitamins, taurine is water-soluble — any excess is simply excreted in urine. Daily supplementation is completely safe for long-term use.
✓
My cat is on a high-quality or raw diet. Does she still need this?›
Yes. Even raw diets benefit from supplementation because taurine degrades upon freezing and processing. Many raw-feeding cat parents use KittySupps as nutritional insurance.
✓
What if my cat already seems healthy? Do I really need to add this?›
Prevention is always easier than treatment. Taurine deficiency develops slowly and silently. By the time symptoms appear, damage has already occurred. Think of taurine like a seatbelt — you don't wait until after the crash.
